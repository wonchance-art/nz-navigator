About

  • The Australian Fresh Produce Alliance was established in 2019 to provide a united voice on cross-industry issues affecting Australia’s fresh produce sector.
  • AFPA brings together leading fresh produce businesses with national scale, deep supply-chain experience and strong connections across growing regions. Its members work across a broad range of commodities and production systems, giving AFPA a practical understanding of the opportunities and challenges facing growers, suppliers, retailers, consumers and government.
  • Since its establishment, AFPA has focused on issues that are best addressed collectively, including workforce, sustainability, packaging, trade, biosecurity, supply-chain resilience, regulatory reform and improving access to fresh fruit and vegetables. Through evidence-based policy, constructive engagement and practical advocacy, AFPA works to support a more competitive, sustainable and resilient fresh produce sector.
AFPA members represent: - Half the turnover of the Australian fresh produce (fruit and vegetables) industry - a quarter of the volume of fresh produce grown in Aust­­­ralia - More than a third of Australian fresh produce exports - More than 20,000 direct employees through peak harvest. - Grower supplier network of more than 1,000 growers
The key issues AFPA is focusing on include: - Workforce: supporting practical policy settings that help fresh produce businesses access the labour and skills needed to supply fresh fruit and vegetables. - Trade and market access: improving access to priority export markets and supporting growth opportunities for Australian fresh produce. - Environmental sustainability: advancing practical sustainability outcomes across the supply chain, including packaging reform, food waste and emissions reporting. - Product integrity: strengthening systems that protect Australian fresh produce, support market confidence and safeguard supply chains. - Production resilience: supporting long-term industry resilience through water security, pollination, research and innovation.

Veronica Papacosta is the Chief Executive Officer of the Australian Fresh Produce Alliance and a respected leader across Australia’s food and agriculture sectors. Previously, she served as CEO and inaugural Chair of Seafood Industry Australia, where she helped establish the organisation as the national voice for the seafood industry and led major initiatives in sustainability, safety and industry collaboration. A third-generation seafood retailer, Veronica brings deep supply chain experience from production to retail. She is a strong advocate for sustainable food systems and effective industry government engagement, and is committed to strengthening Australia’s fresh produce sector.
Cameron Brown is the Head of Policy & Corporate Affairs at AFPA. Cameron supports the AFPA to develop and deliver its priorities and policy agenda. He is an expert at navigating complex policy environments, building relationships across government and industry, and translating strategic priorities into practical outcomes. Before joining AFPA, Cameron spent several years working in government, both within departments and ministerial offices, most recently for a former Premier of Queensland. Having worked across several portfolios, including agriculture, transport, construction, health and more, Cameron brings a broad knowledge base and skill set to AFPA.

AFPA Competition Law Compliance Statement

Version 1 | April 2026
The primary purpose of the AFPA is to serve as the leading voice of the Australian fresh produce sector, domestically and internationally, and to support the sector to be economically, environmentally, and socially sustainable, meeting the needs of Australian and international consumers.
Members wish to make it clear that the sole purpose of its meetings is to discuss matters relevant to achieving the purpose just expressed. Members do not share commercially sensitive information or enter into any discussions, arrangements or any other conduct during these meetings that might be viewed as anti-competitive or otherwise contrary to respective legal obligations. In particular, among other things, members will not discuss during AFPA meetings: - Matters relating to the price of any of our respective products; - acting collectively in relation to any purchase or supply arrangements; - allocating markets, customers, territories or suppliers between or among members; - the terms of any of our respective supply arrangements with any of our customers; - the terms of any of our respective purchasing arrangements with any of our suppliers; or - The coordination of bids or tenders for any contract. If at any point a participant believes a discussion may be moving into prohibited territory, they can raise this immediately, and participants, guided by the Chair, will review the matter and take appropriate corrective action.
